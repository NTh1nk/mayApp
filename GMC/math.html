This page explains the math behind the COMT algorithm.

The COMT algorithm is designed to find the optimal meeting time for a group of people based on their availability.

It uses a combination of linear programming and optimization techniques to determine the best time slot that accommodates the maximum number of participants.

Mathematical Representation

The algorithm can be represented mathematically as follows:

Objective Function: Minimize the total flaw
Constraints: Each participant has a time zone, and the meetings should be scheduled within or as close to their availability timeslots

The solution involves iterating through possible time slots in 5 minute intervals and calculating the total flaw for each slot. The flaw is defined as the sum of the absolute differences between the ideal meeting time and each participant's availability.

Individual flaw: \( F_j = (T - T_j)^2 \)

Minimize total flaw: \( F = \sum F_j \)
The Calculate optimal meeting point (COMP) algorith works in a similar way to the (COMT) algorithm, but works with coordinates and distances instead of time zone and slots. It iterates through a predimined set of cities and finds the optimal one.
